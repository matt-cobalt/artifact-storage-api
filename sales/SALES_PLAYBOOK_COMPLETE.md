## SECTION 1: SALES PROCESS OVERVIEW

### The Cobalt AI Sales Process - Consultative, Fast, High-Close

**Our Sales Advantage:**

Unlike traditional enterprise software sales (12-18 month cycles, $50K+ price points), our sales process is optimized for speed and conversion:

1. **Operational Proof (Not Demo):** We show live production system with 87% conversion, $657K/year value, 99.9% uptime. This eliminates "does it work?" objections.

2. **Week 1 Deployment (Not 12-18 Months):** Live in 7 days, not months. This eliminates "too long to implement" objections.

3. **Capital Efficiency:** $99/month vs $1.35M/year competitors. This eliminates "too expensive" objections.

4. **Mathematical Sophistication:** 125 formulas (Control Theory, Information Theory, Markov Processes). This demonstrates we're not just another AI startup.

5. **Clear ROI:** ROI calculator shows $657K/year value vs $1,188/year cost. This eliminates "not worth it" objections.

### Sales Cycle Timeline

**Total Cycle: 7-14 days from first contact to revenue**

**Day 1: Discovery Call (30 minutes)**
- Qualify lead (Tekmetric user, 10+ calls/day, $300+ average ticket)
- Discover pain points (missed calls, low conversion, after-hours)
- Quantify value ($657K/year opportunity typical)
- Schedule demo if qualified

**Day 2-3: Live Demo (15 minutes)**
- Show OTTO in action (live call)
- Dashboard tour (real metrics)
- Behind the scenes (25 agents, 125 formulas)
- Week 1 deployment preview
- Handle objections
- Close or schedule follow-up

**Day 4-5: Proposal & Negotiation (24 hours)**
- Custom proposal with ROI calculator
- Address final objections
- Trial offer if needed
- Verbal commitment

**Day 6-7: Contract & Deployment Prep**
- Contract signature (simple SaaS agreement)
- Payment setup (Stripe subscription)
- Deployment kickoff (integration setup begins)

**Week 2: Deployment (7 days)**
- Day 1-2: Integrations (Tekmetric, phone system)
- Day 3-4: Testing (validation calls)
- Day 5-7: Go-live (OTTO starts answering)

**Total Time to Value: 14 days from first call to first captured call**

**Why So High?**

1. **Operational Proof Eliminates Risk:** Prospects see real system, real metrics, real results. No "will this work?" uncertainty.

2. **ROI Calculator Shows Value:** $657K/year value vs $99/month cost. Math is undeniable.

## SECTION 2: DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT

### Pre-Call Research (5 minutes before call)

**Required Research:**
- [ ] Company website (services offered, pricing if visible, location)
- [ ] Google Reviews (read 10-15 reviews, identify pain points)
- [ ] LinkedIn (owner background, tenure, business history)
- [ ] Competitive landscape (local competitors, chain presence)
- [ ] Social media (recent posts, customer interactions)

**What to Look For:**
- Pain points from reviews ("couldn't get through", "no one answered", "long wait")
- Owner personality (detail-oriented, tech-forward, traditional)
- Business size (location count, service offerings)
- Local competition (corporate chains, other independents)

**Research Notes Template:**
```
[Company Name]

Location: [City, State]

**Goal:** Build rapport, create urgency, set expectations

**Script:**
```

**Key Points:**
- Be direct (they're busy)
- Lead with proof (Lake Street results)
- Create excitement ($657K/year number)
- Set expectation (questions coming)
- Get permission (make it collaborative)

### Discovery Questions (20 minutes)

#### PAIN DISCOVERY

**Q1: "How many phone calls would you estimate you get per day?"**

*Listen for:* Actual number, estimate, "I don't know"

**Follow-up if they know:**
"That's helpful. How many of those would you say you actually convert to appointments?"

**Follow-up if they don't know:**
"That's okay - a lot of shops don't track it. Rough estimate?"

**Probing questions:**
- "During busy times, do you ever miss calls?"
- "What happens to calls when everyone is busy?"
- "Do you get calls after hours or on weekends?"

*Note:* Most shops underestimate call volume by 20-30%. Use their number, but also ask about busy times and after-hours.

**Q2: "What's your current conversion rate from phone call to scheduled appointment?"**

*Listen for:* "I don't know" (80% of shops), 40-60% (industry average), higher numbers (likely inflated)

**Response if "I don't know":**
"That's totally normal - most shops don't track it. Industry average is 45-60%. That means for every 100 calls, you're booking 45-60 appointments. Sound about right?"

**Response if they give a number:**
"That's [above/below/at] industry average. The shops we work with typically see 50-60% before Cobalt AI, and 87% after. That's usually 30-40 more appointments per month for a shop your size."

*Note:* This is the hook. They'll want to know how we get 87%.

**Q3: "What happens to calls that come in after hours or on weekends?"**

*Listen for:* Voicemail, miss them entirely, call back next day, weekend hours

**Follow-up:**
- "How many after-hours calls would you estimate per week?"
- "What percentage of those turn into appointments when you call back?"
- "Do customers wait for your callback, or do they call competitors?"

*Note:* Usually <20% of after-hours calls convert. This is pure lost revenue we can capture.

**Response:**
"Most shops we talk to say they convert maybe 15-20% of after-hours calls when they call back. The rest go to competitors or forget. We capture 87% of those calls immediately - even at midnight. That alone is worth $50K-100K per year for most shops."

**Q4: "How much time does your team spend on the phone per day?"**

*Listen for:* Hours per day, pulled away from bays, "too much"

**Follow-up:**
- "What would happen if AI handled 80% of those calls?"
- "Would that free up your team to focus on customers in the shop?"
- "What's that worth to you?"

*Note:* Labor cost savings is secondary benefit, but real. $15-25/hour × hours saved = real savings.

**Response:**
"Most shops tell us their team spends 3-4 hours per day on the phone. If AI handled 80% of routine calls - scheduling, pricing, basic questions - that's 2.5 hours per day back to your team. At $20/hour, that's $50/day or $1,500/month in labor savings. Plus they're happier because they're not stuck on the phone."

**Q5: "What's the biggest challenge running your business right now?"**

*Listen for:* Staffing, competition, customer acquisition, margins, call volume, customer service

**Follow-up (connect to solution):**
- If staffing: "What if AI handled routine calls so your team could focus on complex issues?"
- If competition: "What if you captured 100% of calls while competitors miss 30%?"
- If customer acquisition: "What if every call converted at 87% instead of 50%?"
- If margins: "What if you captured $50K/month in revenue you're currently missing?"

*Note:* This is the emotional hook. Connect their biggest pain to our solution.

#### QUALIFICATION

**Q6: "What shop management system do you use?"**

*Must have one of:*
- Tekmetric (best - direct integration)
- Shop-Ware / Shopware (good - API available)
- Mitchell 1 Manager SE (good - API available)
- NAPA Tracs (good - integration available)
- Other (may need custom integration - still qualified)

**Response:**
"Perfect. We integrate directly with [system name]. That means OTTO has access to your customer history, vehicle data, pricing, and schedule in real-time. When a customer calls, OTTO knows their full history instantly. No manual data entry, everything syncs automatically."

*Note:* Integration is critical. If they don't have a shop management system, they're not qualified (too manual, too complex to deploy).

**Q7: "How many calls do you get per day on average?"**

*Threshold:* 10+ calls/day minimum for ROI

**If <10 calls/day:**
"I appreciate your interest. Cobalt AI is designed for shops with 10+ calls per day to make the ROI work. You're at [number]. Let's check back in 6 months when you've grown. Would that work?"

**If ≥10 calls/day:**
"Perfect. That's enough volume to generate real ROI. Let me show you the math..."

*Note:* <10 calls/day = not enough volume. Still be nice, but disqualify gently.

**Q8: "What's your average ticket or service price?"**

*Threshold:* $300+ average for meaningful ROI

**If <$300:**
"Got it. Our system works best for shops with $300+ average tickets. You're at [number]. We can still deploy, but the ROI won't be as dramatic. Would you like to proceed, or wait until you're at $300+ average?"

**If ≥$300:**
"Perfect. That's a great average. Let me show you how that impacts the ROI..."

*Note:* Lower ticket shops can still benefit, but ROI is smaller. Use for calculation.
